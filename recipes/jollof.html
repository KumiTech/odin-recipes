Jollof

Description

Ghanaian jollof rice, spicy tomato-infused rice with peppers, onions, chicken with fried plantains.

Ingredients

  • Jasmine Rice
  • Oil
  • Chicken
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Plantain
  • Spices(Bay Leaves, Curry powder)

Steps

Prepare The Chicken

  1. Season chicken with salt, thyme, curry powder, garlic, ginger, and onions
  2. Steam chicken on low heat for 20-25 minutes to release its natural juices
  3. Add water, cover, and cook for another 35-40 minutes until tender
  4. Strain the stock and reserve the chicken for frying

Prepare The Stew Base

  1. Blend roasted peppers, tomatoes, onions, and scotch bonnet peppers into a smooth paste
  2. Heat oil in a pot, sauté diced onions until fragrant, then add thyme, bay leaves, and seasoning cube
  3. Stir in tomato paste and cook until the oil separates
  4. Add the blended pepper mix and cook for a few minutes

Cook The Jollof Rice

  1. Rinse parboiled rice under cold water until clear
  2. Add rice to the stew base, mixing thoroughly
  3. Add chicken stock, ensuring the liquid covers the rice
  4. Cover pot with aluminum foil and a tight-fitting lid, cooking on low heat for 30-40 minutes

Fry The Plantains

  1. Peel and slice plantains into ½-inch thick diagonal pieces
  2. Heat canola oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat
  3. Fry plantains for 2-3 minutes per side, until golden and crispy
  4. Drain on paper towels

Fry The Chicken

  1. Heat oil in a pan and lightly fry the chicken pieces until golden brown
  2. Alternatively, air-fry or grill for a healthier option

Serving

  1. Serve Jollof rice with fried plantains, grilled or fried chicken, and a side of fresh salad or coleslaw
  2. Garnish with chopped fresh herbs, if desired
